Preventative Maintenance: scheduled routine work to reduce the chance of failure
Predictive Maintenance: using condition data, sensors or trends to service something before it fails
Corrective Maintnenance: fixing an issue that was found before it caused full failure
Reactive Maintenance: repairing only after something breaks
Condition-Based Maintenance: maintenance triggered by actual asset condition, not just time or usage
Routine Maintenance: basic recurring upkeep like cleaning, lubrication, tightening, inspections
Planned Maintenance: any maintenance organized in advance, often including preventative work and planned repairs
Unplanned Maintenance: urgent or unexpected work due to breakdowns or sudden defects
Emergency Maintenance: immediate work needed to address safety, uptime, or damage risk
Deferred Maintenance: needed work that gets postponed
Shutdown / Trunaround Maintenance: major maintenance done while a system or factory is intentionally taken offline
Reliability-Centered Maintenance: choosing the right maintnenance strategy based on consequence and likelihood of failure
Prescriptive Maintenance: analytics not only predict failure, but recommned the best action to take
Autonomous Maintenance: baisc maintenance done by operators, not specialized technicians

A simple way to think about it:

Before Failure: preventative, predictive, condition-based
After Failure: corrective, reactive, emergency
How it's organized: planned, unplanned, deferred, shutdown
How it's prioritized: reliability-centered, risk-based

In facilities and critical assets, the main buckets people usually use are preventative, predictive, corrective, reactive, and emergency.
